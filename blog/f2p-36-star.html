Spiral Abyss F2P Floor 12 Guide

Getting 36 stars in Spiral Abyss as a F2P player is absolutely achievable with the right strategy. This guide covers the current v6.3 lineup, budget team compositions, and chamber-by-chamber tactics.

🎯 Prerequisites

Before attempting Floor 12, make sure you have:

  • Two built teams with at least one main DPS each at level 80/90
  • Key talents at level 8+ for your main DPS and support skills
  • Artifacts at +20 — main stats matter more than set bonuses
  • Weapons at level 80+ — craftable 4-star weapons are good enough
  • AR 45+ — guaranteed 5-star artifact drops from domains
💡 Key insight: Team building is 70% of Abyss success. A well-synergized 4-star team outperforms random 5-star characters every time.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Best F2P Team Compositions

Team A: National Team (Budget)

Xiangling + Xingqiu + Bennett + Sucrose — This is the most famous F2P team and it still clears in v6.3. Xiangling is the damage dealer, Xingqiu enables vape reactions, Bennett heals and buffs, Sucrose groups and shreds res with VV.

  • Xiangling: The Catch (R5), 4pc Emblem, ER 220%+
  • Xingqiu: Sacrificial Sword, 4pc Emblem, ER 200%+
  • Bennett: Sapwood Blade / Favonius, 4pc Noblesse
  • Sucrose: TTDS, 4pc Viridescent Venerer

Team B: Hyperbloom (Budget)

Dendro Traveler + Collei + Barbara + Kuki Shinobu (or Lisa) — Hyperbloom requires almost no investment. Just stack Elemental Mastery on your electro trigger.

  • Dendro Traveler: Sapwood Blade / Favonius, 4pc Deepwood
  • Collei: Favonius / Sacrificial, 4pc Instructor or 2pc Deepwood 2pc ER
  • Barbara: TTDS, 4pc Ocean Hued Clam or Maiden's
  • Kuki Shinobu (or Lisa): Iron Sting (full EM), 2pc +80 EM

🏛️ Chamber 12-1 — Consecrated Beasts

First Half: Consecrated Scorpion + Consecrated Flying Serpent.

  • Focus on the Scorpion first — it has higher HP but is easier to hit
  • Use Sucrose's VV shred to maximize damage windows
  • Keep Bennett's burst up at all times for healing
  • If using National, stagger your bursts so Xiangling's pyronado is up during both beast's vulnerable phases

Second Half: Consecrated Horned Crocodile + Consecrated Vulture.

  • Use Dendro cores to damage both at once
  • Stay mobile — the Crocodile's charge attack is telegraphed but hits hard

🏛️ Chamber 12-2 — Boss Rush

First Half: Algorithm of Semi-Intransient Matrix of Overseer Network (the invisible cube).

  • Bring a Claymore or Geo unit to break the revival prisms
  • When the boss goes invisible, switch to quick-bloom reactions to reveal it faster
  • Timing — save your bursts for when it's vulnerable

Second Half: Aeonblight Drake.

  • Bring a bow character to shoot the weak point on its wing when it flies up
  • Fischl or Kuki (with high EM) shreds its shield quickly
  • Focus on elemental reactions rather than raw damage

🏛️ Chamber 12-3 — The Final Challenge

First Half: Two Ruin Guard + Ruin Grader.

  • Group them together — National team excels at AOE here
  • Bennett's burst ults and Sucrose's grouping make this chamber free
  • Don't waste time chasing individual enemies

Second Half: Setekh Wenut.

  • This boss is the hardest — it spends most of its time underground
  • Only attack when it surfaces (it has 3-4 surface phases)
  • Use Nahida/Collei/DMC marked enemies to keep damaging it even underground
  • Hyperbloom is excellent here — seeds track the boss automatically
  • If you fail stars, reset and retry with better burst alignment

⚡ Retry Strategy

Don't be afraid to reset. If Chamber 1 cost you too much time, restart and adjust your burst rotation. Many players clear each chamber separately:

  • Run Chamber 1 with a battery team to save bursts
  • If Chamber 2 is easier, use your stronger team there
  • Mix and match teams between chambers using retries
🔥 Final tip: Watch your energy before entering each chamber. If your bursts aren't full, wait 10 seconds for cooldowns rather than starting with nothing. That 10 seconds of waiting saves you 30+ seconds of panic.
