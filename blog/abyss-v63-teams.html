Best Teams for Spiral Abyss v6.3 — 36-Star Guide

📅 July 3, 2026 · ✍️ Wiki · 📖 6 min read
← Blog · Abyss Guide
abyss meta guide

The v6.3 Spiral Abyss rotation is here, and it's a tough one. With Consecrated Beasts on Floor 12 Chamber 1 and the double Baptist boss on Chamber 3, you need well-built teams with the right elemental coverage to secure all 36 stars.

In this guide, I'll break down the best team compositions for each half, explain the enemy lineups, and suggest budget alternatives so you can clear regardless of your roster.

Current Blessing & Enemy Lineup

💡 Current Blessing (v6.3): After using an Elemental Skill, the active character gains +75% Elemental DMG for 8s. Stacks up to 2 times.

Floor 12 Chamber 1

First Half: Consecrated Flying Serpent + Consecrated Scorpion (both have 60% Phys RES, 30% All RES)
Second Half: Aeonblight Drake (requires bow or ranged attack to down) + Ruin Guard

Floor 12 Chamber 2

First Half: Frostarm Lawachurl + 2x Cryo Hilichurl Rogues
Second Half: Setekh Wenut (the worm — bring Dendro or Electro)

Floor 12 Chamber 3

First Half: 2x Abyss Herald: Frost Moon (Cryo) + 2x Abyss Herald: Wicked Torrents (Hydro)
Second Half: Baptists (Hydro-Pyro-Electro variant)

🏆 Recommended Teams

First Half — Best Options

🔥 Rational (Raiden National)
Raiden Shogun + Xingqiu + Xiangling + Bennett
Best overall for first half. Xingqiu's rainswords counter the Pyro shields on 12-3. Raiden's burst ignores the Consecrated Beasts' high Phys RES. Energy is never an issue.
🌿 Hyperbloom (Budget King)
Nahida + Xingqiu + Kuki Shinobu + Flex (Zhongli/Alhaitham/Fischl)
Cheapest T0 team that handles both single-target and AoE. Kuki triggers Hyperbloom from off-field, making the Wenut on 12-2 much easier since you don't need precise aim.

Second Half — Best Options

🌊 Neuvillette Hypercarry
Neuvillette + Furina + Kazuha + Zhongli
The current meta king. Neuvillette's charged attack hits the Aeonblight Drake even while flying. His AoE clears the double heralds on 12-3 effortlessly.
❄️ Ayaka Freeze
Ayaka + Shenhe/Rosaria + Kazuha/Sucrose + Kokomi/Mona
Excellent for the second half. Freeze locks down the Wenut during its vulnerability window. Ayaka's burst can snapshot through the entire rotation. Requires good positioning.

⚡ Energy Thresholds

Getting your bursts back on cooldown is critical for this rotation. Here are the recommended ER values:

  • Xiangling: 220%+ ER (solo Pyro), 180%+ with Bennett
  • Xingqiu: 200%+ ER (solo Hydro), 170%+ with Sac Sword
  • Bennett: 200%+ ER
  • Raiden: 230%+ ER for team battery, 270%+ for personal damage
  • Yelan: 200%+ ER (solo), 160%+ with Fav + Xingqiu

💰 F2P Team Recommendations

If you don't have the premium units, here are fully 4-star viable teams for both halves:

First Half: National + Flex
Xiangling + Xingqiu + Bennett + Sucrose/Rosaria/Kaeya
Sucrose provides VV shred and EM share. Rosaria/Kaeya enables reverse melt which works well against the Cryo Shields on 12-3 first half.
Second Half: Hyperbloom + Flex
Dendro MC + Xingqiu + Kuki + Flex (Sucrose/Lisa/Yaoyao)
Dendro MC applies enough Dendro for consistent Blooms. Kuki triggers them all. The bow user (if using Lisa with TTDS) helps down the Aeonblight Drake.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Group the Consecrated Beasts (12-1): Run to one side and wait for the other to charge at you. They group naturally — don't chase them.
  • Aeonblight Drake (12-1 second half): Bring a bow character or use Neuvillette's charged attack. Shoot the core when it's flying to stun it.
  • Wenut timings (12-2): The Wenut has very specific vulnerability windows. Save your big bursts for when it emerges and does the tail slam attack.
  • Herald shields (12-3 first half): Cryo + Hydro shields. Bring Pyro (Xiangling/Bennett) and Electro (Raiden/Beidou/Fischl) to break them fast.
  • Baptist mechanics (12-3 second half): The shield order is Hydro → Pyro → Electro. Bring Hydro + Cryo/Electro + Cryo/Pyro to counter.
🎯 Key takeaway: This rotation favors teams with flexible elemental coverage. Hyperbloom and National remain the safest picks for the first half. Neuvillette and Freeze teams dominate the second half. Build around breaking the 12-3 shields and the rest of the floors will fall into place.
